# Workforce Security Policy

**Document ID:** WF-POL-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** February 1, 2026
**Review Date:** February 1, 2027
**Owner:** HR + Security

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for workforce security, including background checks, training, and acceptable use of Scrybe systems to protect PHI and ensure HIPAA compliance.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and consultants
- Third-party service providers with system access
- Interns and volunteers

## 3. Pre-Employment Requirements

### 3.1 Background Checks

All workforce members with access to PHI must undergo:

| Check Type | Requirement | Renewal |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| **Identity Verification** | Required | At hire |
| **Criminal Background** | Required | At hire + every 3 years |
| **Employment Verification** | Required | At hire |
| **Education Verification** | Role-dependent | At hire |
| **Professional License** | Role-dependent | Annual |

### 3.2 Confidentiality Agreements

Before access to PHI:
- Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- HIPAA Confidentiality Agreement
- Acceptable Use Policy acknowledgment

### 3.3 Role Authorization

Access requests must include:
- Job description
- Justification for PHI access level
- Supervisor approval
- Security team approval for Admin roles

## 4. Security Training

### 4.1 Training Requirements

| Training | Audience | Frequency | Duration |
|----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Security Awareness** | All | Annual | 1 hour |
| **HIPAA Privacy & Security** | PHI access | Annual | 2 hours |
| **Phishing Awareness** | All | Quarterly | 15 min |
| **Role-Specific** | By role | Annual | Varies |
| **Incident Response** | Technical staff | Annual | 1 hour |

### 4.2 Training Timeline

| Event | Training Required | Deadline |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| New hire | Initial security + HIPAA | 30 days |
| Role change | Role-specific | 14 days |
| Policy update | Updated training | 30 days |
| Security incident | Targeted refresher | 7 days |

### 4.3 Training Topics

**Security Awareness:**
- Password security and MFA usage
- Phishing and social engineering
- Physical security
- Mobile device security
- Incident reporting

**HIPAA Training:**
- PHI definition and examples
- Minimum necessary standard
- Individual rights
- Breach notification
- Sanctions for violations

### 4.4 Training Records

Maintain records of:
- Training completion date
- Training content/version
- Assessment scores (if applicable)
- Trainer information

**Retention:** 6 years from employment end

## 5. Acceptable Use

### 5.1 General Principles

- Business use is primary purpose
- Personal use must not interfere with work
- All activity may be monitored
- No expectation of privacy on company systems

### 5.2 Prohibited Activities

| Category | Prohibited Actions |
|----------|-------------------|
| **PHI Handling** | Storing PHI on personal devices, emailing PHI to personal accounts, sharing credentials |
| **System Use** | Installing unauthorized software, bypassing security controls, accessing unauthorized resources |
| **Communication** | Sending confidential info via unsecure channels, discussing PHI in public |
| **Devices** | Connecting unauthorized devices, disabling encryption, jailbreaking |

### 5.3 Email and Communication

- Use company email for business purposes
- Do not send unencrypted PHI via email
- Be cautious of phishing attempts
- Report suspicious emails immediately

### 5.4 Remote Work

Requirements for remote access:
- Company-approved device or VDI
- Secure home network
- Private workspace (screen not visible to others)
- MFA enabled
- No PHI on personal printers

### 5.5 Social Media

- Do not discuss work involving PHI
- Do not post photos of workplace with visible PHI
- Follow company social media policy
- Report any PHI exposure immediately

## 6. Access Termination

### 6.1 Voluntary Termination

| Timing | Action | Owner |
|--------|--------|-------|
| 2 weeks notice | Plan access removal | Supervisor + IT |
| Last day | Disable accounts | IT |
| Last day | Collect equipment | IT |
| Last day | Remove physical access | Facilities |
| 1 week after | Verify all access removed | Security |

### 6.2 Involuntary Termination

| Timing | Action | Owner |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Immediately | Disable all accounts | IT |
| Immediately | Revoke all active sessions | IT |
| Immediately | Collect equipment | Security |
| Immediately | Remove physical access | Facilities |
| Same day | Review audit logs | Security |

### 6.3 Exit Procedures

- Return all company equipment
- Return all access badges/keys
- Sign acknowledgment of ongoing confidentiality obligations
- Exit interview (optional)
- Confirm no PHI on personal devices

## 7. Sanctions

### 7.1 Violation Categories

| Category | Examples | Potential Sanctions |
|----------|----------|---------------------|
| **Minor** | Forgetting to lock screen, weak password | Verbal warning |
| **Moderate** | Sharing credentials, improper PHI handling | Written warning |
| **Serious** | Unauthorized access, policy circumvention | Suspension, termination |
| **Severe** | Intentional breach, data theft | Termination, legal action |

### 7.2 Sanction Process

1. Investigation and documentation
2. Severity determination
3. Appropriate sanction applied
4. Documentation in personnel file
5. Appeal process (if applicable)

### 7.3 Legal Consequences

HIPAA violations may result in:
- Civil penalties: $100 - $50,000 per violation
- Criminal penalties: Up to $250,000 and 10 years imprisonment
- State law penalties (varies by state)

## 8. Reporting Requirements

### 8.1 What to Report

- Security incidents (actual or suspected)
- Policy violations observed
- Phishing attempts
- Lost or stolen devices
- Unusual system behavior
- Requests to violate policy

### 8.2 How to Report

| Method | Contact |
|--------|---------|
| Email | security@scrybe.app |
| Slack | #security-incidents |
| Phone | [Security hotline] |
| Anonymous | [Anonymous reporting tool] |

### 8.3 Non-Retaliation

- Good faith reporting is protected
- No retaliation for reporting in good faith
- Retaliation itself is a violation
